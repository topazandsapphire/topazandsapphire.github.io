]]> By: Angelique Jurd https://topazandsapphire.github.io/how-to-get-out-of-a-career-rut/#comment-125 Mon, 14 Sep 2015 20:49:42 +0000 https://topazandsapphire.github.io/?p=528#comment-125 Toward the end of my corporate career as I became increasingly dissatisfied with working for someone else (I’m not good at taking orders it turns out after all these years)I decided to go back to school part time – while keeping my job. I was in a total rut but simply applying for jobs wasn’t helping because I knew the problem was as much with what I wanted as it was the job I had – and simply changing location wasn’t going to solve the issue. Within two months of beginning my study I had quit my job and was studying and freelancing full time. It was the best decision I ever made. By opting to study part time I gave myself enough of a distraction from the career frustration to actually *think* about what I wanted to do next. Since then, I’ve spoken with several colleagues and found that’s what they needed too – not necessarily to go back to school – but something to remove their focus from the “this is all wrong and I hate it” long enough to figure out “I don’t like this, I do like that, and I want to be ….” after which it was less challenging to begin taking steps toward a solution.
Great post – thanks.
