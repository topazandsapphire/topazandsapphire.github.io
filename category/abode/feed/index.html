]]> Spring is in full swing and it’s time to dust away the cobwebs of winter and embrace the sunshine. One of my favorite things about “spring fever” is getting a fresh start in my home. Here are 5 ways to spruce up your space for spring.

1. Add greenery/fresh flowers

Is there anything better than having some fresh flowers on your table or mantel? (Aside from a dozen hot, glazed Krispy Kreme donuts). They just make me happy. Fresh flowers every week can get pricey, but you don’t have to shop the farmer’s market every weekend to bring the outside in. All the plants in my house except for one are *cough* … fake. (Including those succulents on that window sill). My lower-level apartment, which is very dark most of the day, isn’t the best environment for plants to thrive. The idea is to bring the cheeriness of springtime indoors, so go to Target or Michael’s and buy the best looking faux’s you can!

 

2. Switch out throw pillows & blankets

Throw pillows and blankets are a great way to add festive and seasonal charm. Think of furniture as a canvas and accessories like pillows and blankets as the paint for the kind of tone you want to set for your home for the season.

 

3. Brighten up kitchen table with a runner or placemats

Kitchen tables kind of feel naked to me without a runner or placemats. This is another simple way to bring the cheeriness of springtime right to the table in fun patterns and bright colors.

 

4. Clean out & organize your closet

PHOTO SOURCE// Bonnie Tsang, Design Love Fest

I typically put my winter clothes and boots into storage in the off season. Every time spring rolls around and I pull out my brightly colored tops and floral dresses, I make sure to go through my entire arsenal of clothing and giveaway or sell what I know I won’t be wearing this spring or next winter. It gives my closet room to breath and my wardrobe a fresh feeling.

 

5. Revamp your bookshelves

Over time we tend to accumulate things and just find a place for them. I do this a lot with books and knick knacks. Some books I buy, I’ll never read again and they just take up prime real estate in my book shelf. Other times, you may grow out of picture frames or accessories you once liked and it’s time to display things that are more “you.” I tend to revamp my bookshelf once every few months to give it a new look, a fresh take, and to weed out what I won’t be reading or loving anymore. This is how my bookshelf looked a few months ago.

Happy Springtime Sprucing! We would love to see your “shelfies,” so make sure to mention/tag us on Instagram at @topazandsapphire so we can see how you all styled your bookshelves for spring.

]]> Once you move out of the dorms and into a real apartment/home of your own it’s time to buy some of those kitchen essentials you’ve been lusting after at all of your favorite stores (ahem I’m looking at you Anthropologie). Once you get all the necessities – glasses, silverware, plates, etc., it’s time to invest in some of the kitchen gadgets that will make your kitchen fully functional. Here are four kitchen gadgets that we think every woman needs.

1. Mixer

PHOTO SOURCE//  Sarah Postman

A KitchenAid is at the top of every girl’s wedding registry and/or Christmas list. If you spend a lot of time baking or you’re in the kitchen in general, a KitchenAid is well worth the investment. A high quality mixer will make life so much easier for you, especially with the variety of attachments! If you’re looking for a more affordable option, a handheld mixer will do the trick just fine too.

2. Large Cutting Board

PHOTO SOURCE// Half Baked Harvest

Using a cutting board for cutting or simple food prep, like making a sandwich, makes clean up a breeze. Not only that, but you also save your precious countertops from getting scratched and bruised. Whether you opt for a classic wooden butcher block or a simpler cutting mat like this, having a large cutting board is something you won’t regret. I use mine every single day!

3. Chopper

PHOTO SOURCE// The Pioneer Woman

This thing is small but mighty and saves so much time when cooking. As someone who eats a lot of veggies and cooks with vegetables often, the chopper makes dicing garlic, onions, bell peppers, carrots, and more a complete breeze. It’s also perfect for crushing nuts.

4. Blender

PHOTO SOURCE// Minimalistic Baker

Everyone has their favorite brand of blender – Ninja versus Blendtec. Forget the name brands, the most important thing is that you invest in a blender with a strong blade. You can use your blender all year round – smoothies in the summer and soup in the winter. Blenders are also great for protein shakes, daiquiris, and hummus making.

The post Making A Simple Brunch, Soiree Brilliant appeared first on Topaz and Sapphire.

]]> Saturday brunch is a favorite in our household and is something I look forward to almost every weekend. This past weekend I decided to host a Valentine’s Soiree & Brunch at my home. I really wanted this brunch to feel more like a party, and less like your everyday Saturday brunch (although going out for brunch is always such a treat!). I didn’t want to spend a million dollars for the small event, but I wanted my guests to feel like a million bucks. Here are a few tips on how you can make a simple brunch, soiree brilliant!

 

Decor

Instead of the usual place setting, add a little something extra to your table or home decor that fits with the theme of your party. Little details can go a long way. With a sprinkle of heart confetti, a beautiful feminine bouquet from Trader Joes, name tags, and extra pops of Valentine’s colors on the table, I was able to take my tablescape from everyday to soiree brilliant!

Food

Any event, big or small, requires some sort of menu. Of course with most of the parties I host, food is the main attraction (hence, brunch), but it doesn’t always need to be the main focus!

Presentation

With any soiree or get together, presentation is key! Presentation begins with the invitation. While some may think the use of paper invitations is extinct, I feel like sending invites in the mail adds a special touch to any party, way before the event even begins!

In addition, presenting your food, activities, home, tablescape, decor, etc. can really help your guests feel really special. The small details, like the mini pancakes in the paper ramekins or the heart stamped paper straws, don’t go unnoticed!

Activity & The Take-Away

To make a get-together more of a party, implement some sort of activity or game into the event schedule. An activity helps unite the guests and ensures that everyone feels included. Near the close of my Valentine’s Soiree & Brunch, we all passed our name cards, which doubled as bookmarks, in a circle and wrote what we loved most about each person. It was fitting to the event and it gave everyone a little something to take home so they could remember their 2016 Valentine’s Day!

I also sent each couple home with a bag full of their favorite chocolates and a couples Valentine’s card game. Party favors are never required, but are always so much appreciated by your guests, especially if they are heartfelt.

Making a simple brunch, soiree brilliant isn’t as hard as it seems! Stick to these few tips and you’re on your way to making your next get-together with your friends, soiree perfect.
